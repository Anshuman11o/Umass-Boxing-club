Club History

The UMass Boxing Club was founded in 2007 by President Tom McDevitt and VP Regina Diemand. In 2010, the competition team was established by Coach Rocky Snow, a Marine Corps Vietnam War veteran with over 40 years of boxing experience. Within 2 years, Coach Rocky guided Josue Lopez to a National Championship.

Josue was a three-time NCBA All-American and won Gold at the 2012 and 2013 National Championships (132lbs), in addition to serving as club president and team captain.

After Coach Rocky's retirement, the team is now led by co-head coaches and UMass Boxing alums, Sean Donnelly and Meiya Berkey. Both are standout competitors with a decade of experience and mentorship under Coach Rocky. Their aim is to continue his legacy by helping members succeed in both the ring and the classroom.

Members learn fundamental boxing techniques and engage in cardio-based exercises under professional supervision. The club aims to promote healthy lifestyles, a sense of community, and most importantly, fun!

Many UMass Boxing alumni credit their current success and friendships to their time in the club.

Coach Rocky Snow

Rocky Snow began boxing in 1975 at the Holyoke Boys and Girls Club. Despite starting late (24 years old), he quickly made a name for himself by winning the New England Golden Gloves at 126 pounds in early 1976. As a Marine and Vietnam War veteran, boxing was the perfect outlet for him.

Moving to the professional ranks the following year, Rocky fought 26 times over a decade. In the 1980s, he transitioned to a coaching role, mentoring many kids in the Holyoke and Springfield areas. His fighters won various titles and accolades, but safety and self-improvement remained his top priorities.

Rocky continued coaching at Westover Air Reserve Base, volunteering and training armed forces personnel in weight lifting and general fitness. His collegiate coaching career spanned multiple decades, including tenures at Western New England University and Central Connecticut State University. From 2010 to 2022, he coached UMass Boxing, guiding over 70 collegiate boxers, including NCBA National champions and All-Americans.

Overall, Coach Rocky has dedicated nearly half a century to the sport as a fighter, coach, and mentor, with a steadfast focus on keeping his fighters safe and helping them become champions both inside and outside the ring.
